Professional Self-Assessment

As I near the completion of my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science at Southern New Hampshire University, I have developed a clear and focused professional identity: I aspire to become a Data Application Developer. This self-assessment reflects on my academic journey, the skills I have strengthened through coursework and the capstone ePortfolio, and how I am now prepared to contribute meaningfully in a professional technology environment.

Throughout the program, I have grown from someone who could write functional code into a developer who understands how to design, optimize, and scale real-world data-driven applications. The most transformative experience has been the CS 499 capstone, where I took a single artifact — the Grazioso Salvare Animal Rescue Dashboard from CS 340 — and progressively enhanced it across three critical categories: Software Design and Engineering, Algorithms and Data Structures, and Databases. This iterative enhancement process allowed me to demonstrate measurable growth while building a cohesive portfolio that directly supports my career goals.

Key Skills Developed

One of the most valuable skills I developed is the ability to collaborate effectively in team environments and communicate technical decisions to both technical and non-technical stakeholders. Through QSO 355 (Project Management), I gained hands-on experience creating Work Breakdown Structures, Gantt charts, risk registers, and stakeholder registers. These projects required me to work collaboratively with team members, manage scope, and present information in ways that support organizational decision-making. This experience strengthened my ability to contribute in structured, team-based environments — a skill that is essential for any Data Application Developer working on cross-functional projects.

In software engineering and design, I significantly strengthened my abilities by refactoring the original monolithic Jupyter Notebook into a professional Flask MVC application. This enhancement required me to apply separation of concerns, modular architecture, and clean routing. I also improved code readability by adding meaningful comments and documentation throughout the codebase — skills that are essential for building maintainable production systems and supporting collaborative development.

My work in algorithms and data structures focused on performance optimization. By implementing MongoDB compound indexes and creating a performance benchmarking endpoint, I demonstrated the ability to analyze time complexity, identify bottlenecks, and apply appropriate data structures to improve efficiency.

The databases enhancement further deepened my expertise. I replaced client-side data processing with MongoDB aggregation pipelines, moving computation to the database layer. This approach is more efficient, scalable, and aligned with industry best practices.

Artifacts & Growth

The three enhancements in this ePortfolio are intentionally cohesive. They all build upon the same core artifact and progressively add layers of professionalism, performance, and analytical capability. Together, they tell a clear story of growth: from a working prototype to a well-architected, optimized, and analytically powerful data application.

While I have made strong progress across most course outcomes, I recognize that developing a security mindset remains an area for continued growth. Although I considered basic input validation during development, I did not implement authentication or authorization in this version. However, through my coursework and self-reflection, I have become more aware of the importance of secure coding practices. I now understand the need to validate all user inputs, protect against common vulnerabilities such as SQL injection and cross-site scripting, and implement proper access controls. Moving forward, I plan to strengthen this area by incorporating secure authentication mechanisms, role-based access control, and following secure coding standards in future projects. I believe developing a strong security mindset is essential for any developer working with sensitive data.

Looking Ahead

Completing this capstone has given me both confidence and clarity. I am now prepared to enter the workforce with a portfolio that showcases real, production-oriented skills in full-stack development, database optimization, and data-driven application design. I am particularly excited to apply these abilities in roles that involve building intelligent, scalable data applications. I am also considering pursuing certifications such as the AWS Certified Data Engineer to further strengthen my profile.

In summary, the CS 499 ePortfolio represents the culmination of my undergraduate journey. It demonstrates my ability to take an idea from concept to a polished, functional application while continuously improving its design, performance, and analytical power. I am proud of the work I have produced and am eager to bring these skills into a professional setting where I can continue learning and delivering value as a Data Application Developer.
