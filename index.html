Professional Self-Assessment

As I near the completion of my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science at Southern New Hampshire University, I have developed a clear and focused professional identity: I aspire to become a Data Application Developer. This self-assessment reflects on my academic journey, the skills I have strengthened through coursework and the capstone ePortfolio, and how I am now prepared to contribute meaningfully in a professional technology environment.

Throughout the program, I have grown from someone who could write functional code into a developer who understands how to design, optimize, and scale real-world data-driven applications. The most transformative experience has been the CS 499 capstone, where I took a single artifact — the Grazioso Salvare Animal Rescue Dashboard from CS 340 — and progressively enhanced it across three critical categories: Software Design and Engineering, Algorithms and Data Structures, and Databases.

Key Skills Developed

One of the most valuable skills I developed is the ability to collaborate effectively in team environments and communicate technical decisions to both technical and non-technical stakeholders. Through QSO 355 (Project Management) and multiple group-oriented assignments, I learned how to create clear documentation, manage scope, and present technical solutions in ways that support organizational decision-making.

In software engineering and design, I significantly strengthened my abilities by refactoring the original monolithic Jupyter Notebook into a professional Flask MVC application. This enhancement required me to apply separation of concerns, modular architecture, and clean routing — skills that are essential for building maintainable production systems.

My work in algorithms and data structures focused on performance optimization. By implementing MongoDB compound indexes and creating a performance benchmarking endpoint, I demonstrated the ability to analyze time complexity, identify bottlenecks, and apply appropriate data structures to improve efficiency.

The databases enhancement further deepened my expertise. I replaced client-side data processing with MongoDB aggregation pipelines, moving computation to the database layer. This approach is more efficient, scalable, and aligned with industry best practices.

Artifacts & Growth

The three enhancements in this ePortfolio are intentionally cohesive. They all build upon the same core artifact and progressively add layers of professionalism, performance, and analytical capability. Together, they tell a clear story of growth: from a working prototype to a well-architected, optimized, and analytically powerful data application.

While I have made strong progress across most course outcomes, I recognize that security remains an area for continued growth. I plan to strengthen this area in future projects by incorporating secure coding practices and proper authentication mechanisms.

Looking Ahead

Completing this capstone has given me both confidence and clarity. I am now prepared to enter the workforce with a portfolio that showcases real, production-oriented skills in full-stack development, database optimization, and data-driven application design. I am particularly excited to apply these abilities in roles that involve building intelligent, scalable data applications.

I am proud of the work I have produced and am eager to bring these skills into a professional setting where I can continue learning and delivering value as a Data Application Developer.
